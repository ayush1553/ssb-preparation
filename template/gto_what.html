🎯 Basis of Group Testing 🧠👥

Group Testing is based on the Gestalt Theory 🧩 — which suggests a person’s behavior is better judged in a group than alone!

🌀 The GTO (Group Testing Officer):

  • 👀 First observes you individually
  • 🕵️ Then watches you in a group
  • 🔍 Finally gives a close observation to assess your personality

There are a total of 9 activities, grouped into three main series:

📘 1. Basic Series 🧱

💥 Includes 4 key tasks:

  • 🗣️💬 Group Discussion (GD) – 2 Rounds
  • 📋🧠 Group Planning Exercise (GPE)
  • 🧗‍♂️🔧 Progressive Group Task (PGT)
  • 🏃‍♂️🪵 Group Obstacle Race (GOR)

🔄 3 Phases of Development:

  • 🌱 Familiarity: Explore & interact
  • 💪 Self-Assertion: Try to lead or dominate
  • 🤝 Cooperation: Start working as a team
👉 Teamwork is KEY!
If you can't blend into the group, even if you're talented alone, ❌ you may fail.

📗 2. Confirmatory Series ✅

Contains 4 more tasks to check your individual performance 🔍:

  • 🧍‍♂️🧍‍♂️ Half Group Task (HGT)
  • 🎤📚 Lecturette (Lec)
  • 🏃‍♂️🔥 Individual Obstacles (IO)
  • 👨‍✈️🎯 Command Task (CT)

🌟 In this series, the GTO observes you more closely as an individual.

📙 3. Final Series 🎯

🧪 Only 1 task:

  • 🫱🫲 Final Group Task (FGT)

This is the GTO's final chance to observe you in a group before concluding the testing.

💡 Important: Do well in all series, especially the basic series.
😬 Poor performance there makes it hard to recover.
✅ A strong start gives confidence throughout.

📝 Final Thoughts 💭

  • 🔄 Be adaptive
  • 🧭 Show leadership
  • 🤝 Be a team player
  • 😎 Stay confident and calm
