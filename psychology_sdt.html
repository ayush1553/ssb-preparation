🌟 Self-Description Test (SDT) 🌟

📝 Introduction

In this section, candidates are given 15 minutes⏱️ and asked to write five different paragraphs describing:

  1. What their parents think of them 👨‍👩‍👧
  2. What their teachers/employers think of them 👨‍🏫👩‍💼
  3. What their friends/colleagues think 🤝
  4. What they think of themselves 💭
  5. What kind of person they want to become or what improvements they want 🌱✨
⚠️ Remember: You must project your true qualities while staying realistic. Avoid exaggerations 🚫. If you claim to be a top student 📚 but have low marks, it won’t be believable ❌.

💡 Key Tip

  • Be realistic but positive ✅
  • Highlight your strengths, but acknowledge areas of improvement too 🔍
  • Don't just write what sounds good – write what is good about you! 🌈
📘 Illustration Example

👪 Parents' View
My parents think I am a bright child 🌟 with lots of skills. They believe I can balance both academics 📚 and outdoor activities 🏃‍♂️ equally well. They know I’m dependable and trust me with any task.

👩‍🏫 Teachers' View
My teachers say I’m sincere and enthusiastic, especially in organizing events 🗓️. They think I am good at working in teams 🤝 and show leadership qualities. However, they suggest I balance studies and outdoor interests better 📖⚖️.

🧑‍🤝‍🧑 Friends' View
My friends love me for being helpful 💞 and sincere. They find me trustworthy 🔒, fun to be around 🎉, and share their problems with me. They describe me as someone simple and dependable.

🪞 Self-Image
I believe I am sincere, caring 💖, and responsible. I may not top in academics, but I work hard in areas I love. I'm friendly, dynamic ⚡, and respect elders. I live simply and enjoy each moment ☀️.

🎯 The Kind of Person I Want to Become
I aim to become a successful person through hard work and determination 💪. Since I love outdoor activities and teamwork, I aspire to join the armed forces 🇮🇳. I want to be known for my sincerity, professionalism, and leadership 👨‍✈️.
